PunchedMono
This typeface draws from the structural rhythm of punched cards, one of the earliest ways humans stored information through absence. Instead of drawing lines or strokes, each letter takes shape by removing parts of a grid. A character is defined not by what is added, but by what is taken away. Absence is the principle here. A hole becomes a signal. Empty space carries meaning. The grid stays constant; only the removals differ, and from those removals, language emerges.
